%%%%%%%%% ABSTRACT
\begin{abstract}
As large language models (LLMs) are deployed as autonomous agents, the ability to persist long-horizon conversational context has become a critical bottleneck. Bounded context windows, high inference costs, and rigid local storage constraints require memory systems to be highly efficient. Traditional extractive summarization methods like TF-IDF treat conversations as unordered bags of words, often destroying sequential dependencies and discarding critical architectural pivots. To address this, we introduce Temporal Semantic Guided Compression (TSGC), an algorithm that explicitly optimizes for storage-efficient conversational memory. TSGC computes smooth temporal decay alongside a dense $N \times N$ semantic future dependency graph, allowing it to aggressively purge conversational filler while preserving core decision pivots. Instead of optimizing for maximum compression, TSGC allows for precise calibration along a Pareto trade-off curve between information loss and disk storage savings. Our results on the WildChat corpus demonstrate that TSGC-AT yields statistically significant storage savings over TF-IDF while maintaining superior Question-Answering (QA) and Pivot Recall accuracy at equivalent compression thresholds.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Autonomous agents driven by Large Language Models (LLMs) require persistent context to maintain coherent long-term interactions. However, continuously growing conversational histories quickly exceed bounded context windows and inflate inference costs. Furthermore, local agent deployments and browser extensions impose strict limits on disk storage, necessitating highly compressible memory formats.

Existing extractive baselines, such as TF-IDF, preserve keyword density but destroy the sequential nature of the dialogue. This results in disjointed fragments that fail to retain recent instructions or protect early architectural decisions. 

In this paper, we propose \textbf{Temporal Semantic Guided Compression (TSGC)}, an algorithm that explicitly models the future dependency and semantic novelty of every message. TSGC dynamically identifies high-value utterances—such as architectural decisions, novel constraints, and immediate user instructions—while aggressively compressing conversational filler. 

We evaluate TSGC against standard baselines across a rigorous benchmark, demonstrating superior performance on the Pareto trade-off curve between Storage Savings (\%) and Pivot Preservation.

\section{Methodology}
TSGC operates as an extractive compression pipeline. We propose three variants to isolate the incremental value of our architectural enhancements:

\subsection{TSGC Base: Temporal Decay \& Dependency}
Unlike static sliding windows, TSGC applies a smooth exponential temporal decay function. To prevent the loss of critical early context, it computes an $N \times N$ cosine similarity matrix and assigns a \textit{Future Dependency Score} to each message based on its semantic influence over the remainder of the conversation.

\subsection{TSGC-AG: Semantic Novelty}
TSGC-AG incorporates \textit{Semantic Novelty} to rescue early context. Novelty is calculated as $1.0 - \max(\text{similarity to previous messages})$. If an early message introduces a completely novel semantic concept, it receives a retention boost, dynamically protecting unrepeated core constraints.

\subsection{TSGC-AT: Full Architecture}
TSGC-AT (the full algorithm) incorporates zero-shot classification via dense embeddings to identify \textit{Conversation Acts} (e.g., Decisions, Requirements) and explicit semantic \textit{Pivots}. The final retention score is a parameterized formula combining Temporal Decay (20\%), Future Dependency (30\%), Semantic Novelty (20\%), Acts (15\%), and Pivots (15\%). The algorithm sweeps across a parameterized threshold to generate a continuous quality-efficiency trade-off curve.

\section{Experiments and Results}
We evaluate TSGC on an architectural decision dataset and the large-scale WildChat corpus.

\subsection{Quality vs Compression Efficiency Trade-off}
TSGC allows for targeted calibration of information preservation. The Pareto frontier demonstrates that at identical storage budgets, TSGC-AT consistently yields higher QA accuracy and Pivot Recall than TF-IDF. This proves that semantic dependency is a superior predictor of long-term memory utility than keyword frequency.

\subsection{Architectural Pivot Preservation}
Complex tasks involve distinct decision points (pivots). Our evaluation reveals that early pivots are often sacrificed by naive compression. TSGC-AT successfully identifies and recalls the vast majority of ground-truth architectural pivots by recognizing their persistent semantic shadow throughout the remainder of the conversation.

\subsection{Storage Optimization at Scale (WildChat)}
TSGC outputs are semantically coherent and heavily deduplicated. When standard DEFLATE compression is applied, TSGC achieves massive, statistically significant ($p < 0.01$) storage savings compared to TF-IDF. This makes TSGC an ideal fit for local, storage-constrained agent architectures. 

\section{Conclusion}
TSGC provides a scalable, storage-efficient memory mechanism for long-horizon LLM agents. By explicitly modeling future dependency, semantic novelty, and conversational acts, it establishes a new Pareto-optimal frontier, preserving actionable agent memory while drastically reducing storage footprints.
